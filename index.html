webStair Group Program for Veterans

Purpose and goals

  • Help Veterans who have symptoms of PTSD
  • Develop coping skills in a safe environment
  • Develop skills to increase emotional regulation, reduce trauma-related symptoms, and help members build a supportive peer community

Setting

  • Setting will be telehealth
  • Ten week self-led sessions
  • Move at your own pace
  • Bi-weekly group meetings to discuss progress and goals
  • Group meetings are optional
  • Therapist led with peer support coach assistance

Group Composition

  • Closed format
  • Members will be veterans with symptoms of PTSD
  • 20-25 members
  • Age of members should be between 18-55 year-old

Eligibility

  • Designed for veterans who have experienced trauma or are currently experiencing symptoms of PTSD.
  • Veterans will need to have internet access and a desire to enhance emotional regulation skills.

Recruitment

  • Local VA facilities
  • Community-based veteran services
  • Outpatient clinics
  • Referrals from mental health providers

Screening

  • Completion of a brief intake process is required. The intake process will be a clinical interview nd standardized screening with online PHQ-8 and PCL-5 assessments that may be later uploaded to selected members’ Veteran Dashboard to track progress throughout the program (Ennis et al., 2021)
  • These will assess trauma history, current mental health symptoms, motivation for treatment, and readiness for a skill-based, partially self guided program

Length, Frequency, and Duration of Meetings

  • Ten weeks of self-guided modules
  • Five bi-weekly telehealth group sessions
  • Each session will last 60-75 minutes
  • Members are encouraged to work through one to two modules while not in a group setting

Rules and Procedures

  • Voluntary Self-Termination- Group members are empowered to self-terminate for any reason they believe is necessary.Group leaders will provide proper referrals for continued treatment and information for future groups that the member can start back with the program if they desire at a later time.
  • Closed Group-The group is a closed support group; no new members can join after the initial meeting.
  • Group sessions are biweekly. Group sessions are optional, but members are encouraged to join to share progress and practice skills with the other members.
  • Group confidentiality and mutual respect will be discussed so that group members can opt into a group confidentiality pact and maintain a safe and respectful online space.

Ethical Considerations

  • Online security is up to date and in compliance with HIPAA and VA standards (Veteran Affairs, 2013).
  • As part of completing the welcome module, participants must complete the introduction process that reviews and requires electronic signature of informed consent for acknowledging webSTAIR’s group confidentiality agreement that outlines the group purpose, behavioral expectations, and limits of confidentiality (Roddy et al., 2024).
  • Trauma-informed group facilitators monitor for emotional distress and follow crisis protocols and referrals, as needed. Additionally, group meetings formally address informed consent and confidentiality as part of the introduction each live session.
